About the Project

A remake of Jumping Jack, the 1983 ZX Spectrum arcade game originally published by Imagine Software. The original's challenge is preserved intact: navigate upward through rows of platforms, clear gaps with precise jumps, and avoid enemies with deliberately minimal controls (arrow keys to move and jump, Enter to start).

The project began as a personal exercise in extracting the design of a classic game through reverse engineering and reproducing it faithfully. Available on itch.io as a free Windows download.

Technical Highlights

  • Minimal, intentional control scheme matching the original (arrow keys + Enter)
  • Reverse-engineered design from playing the original

Features

  • Faithful recreation of the Jumping Jack ZX Spectrum arcade game, including the small lore between levels

What I Learned

Remaking a classic is a useful design exercise precisely because you cannot take shortcuts: every mechanic has to be understood well enough to reproduce it accurately. Reverse-engineering how the original handled platform rows, enemy movement, and gap detection built an intuition for classic arcade design principles that is harder to acquire from reading about them.
