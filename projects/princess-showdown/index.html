About the Project

Princess Showdown is a 2D platformer submitted to the Indie Game Making Contest 2018, built solo in Unity. Princess Rose has been thrown into her rival Serena's dungeon and must fight her way back to reclaim her crown. The core mechanic is a dual magic form system: players switch between two distinct modes to handle different enemy types and platforming hazards, with roughly 30 minutes of content from entry to crown.

It placed 43rd out of 281 in the People's Choice Vote. Keyboard and gamepad are both supported, with gamepad recommended. I published a Prototype Postmortem devlog after release, reflecting on what worked and what did not.

Technical Highlights

  • Two switchable magic forms that change combat approach and traversal options
  • Skeletal 2D animation for the player character
  • Virtual cameras and parallax backgrounds for environmental depth
  • Event sequencing system for in-gameplay cutscenes
  • Prototype Postmortem devlog published post-release

Features

  • Enemy encounters and environmental hazards
  • Approximately 30 minutes of gameplay
  • Keyboard and gamepad support (gamepad recommended)

What I Learned

This project introduced several technical challenges tackled for the first time: skeletal 2D animation, virtual cameras, parallax backgrounds, and event sequencing for cutscenes that occur mid-gameplay. The on-the-fly ability switching also required careful state management to keep transitions clean and responsive. Writing a public postmortem after release added a layer of reflection that sharpened the ability to assess design decisions critically.
