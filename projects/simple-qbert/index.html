About the Project

Simple QBert recreates the first level of Q*bert, the 1982 Gottlieb arcade classic. The original is a puzzle-action game where the player hops across an isometric pyramid of cubes, changing their colors while avoiding enemies.

This was developed as a class assignment with a two-week window. It was completed in two days. That speed and fidelity led directly to a recommendation from the teacher, which landed me a position at Illuminia Studios. Playable free in the browser, no download needed.

What I Learned

Reverse-engineering a classic game forces you to understand its systems at a fundamental level. The isometric cube-hopping, enemy routing, and color state logic all had to be understood precisely to reproduce them correctly. Finishing well ahead of schedule demonstrated that thorough problem decomposition matters more than time budgeted, and the professional opportunity that followed reinforced that publicly sharing quality work opens doors.
