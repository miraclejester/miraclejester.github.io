About

I'm a software developer who has worked across game development and web, examples of my work are combat systems for an educational MMO used in schools across North America, shipping Metroidvania jam games that placed in the top 10% of their respective competitions, and owning client-side monetization systems that drove measurable profit improvements in A/B testing

My game work lives primarily in Godot, PixiJS and Unity. In Godot I've built node-based state machines, custom GLSL shaders, FMOD audio integrations, and player controllers that require both technical precision and a strong sense of game feel. With PixiJS I've shipped professional live-service features to millions of players at Prodigy. With Unity I designed and built the data-driven terminal systems powering VIZARD, contributed across a large coordinated team for ForgeFront and more.

I've operated at two ends of the game development spectrum: solo jam programmer designing systems from scratch under a one-week deadline, and professional developer shipping to a live audience of millions with the full weight of QA, product, and cross-team coordination. That range makes me comfortable with both ambiguity and process. I'm equally at home owning a feature end-to-end or contributing as one developer on a large coordinated team.
